Biography

Growing up in Shanghai, one of the largest yet fastest-growing cities on earth, I have seen urban studies and geography in action throughout my life. I have been particularly fascinated by the rapid expansion of the city’s transport network, which grew by 17 times in size within 20 years. The influx of people into the city is putting intense pressure on this network despite its unprecedented expansion rate, and this tension exemplifies the appeal of urban studies as an academic subject.

My enthusiasm for urban studies led me to take up an internship at the Chinese Academy of Science. During this time, I worked in the transport geography group, where I helped with the processing of spatiotemporal “big data” using GIS. The project culminated in me co-authoring a paper about healthcare accessibility.

In one of my projects, I looked at the spatiotemporal heterogeneity and interrelationships of transit accessibility and deprivation in multiple UK cities. I used the network and spatial analyst toolbox in ArcGIS to process and analyse socioeconomic and transport network data, gathered from various sources (e.g., census, OSM, POI data). In doing so, I found a spatially autocorrelated pattern of accessibility and a quadratic relationship between accessibility and deprivation.

Beyond urban studies, I am also passionate about climate change and sustainability. Notably, I worked in the Action for Climate Empowerment (ACE) working group of YOUNGO, the youth constituency of the UNFCCC. I organised the ACE Youth Forum, an 8-day event that connected almost 800 people from 50+ countries. In recognition of my efforts, the UN envoy on youth selected me from 8700+ applicants to be a delegate to the Pre-COP26 youth summit. In July 2021, the effects of climate change were brought to the fore when Central China suffered from flooding caused by extreme storms. To help with the relief effort, I developed a Web GIS with geolocated hazards data. A sense of social responsibility was raised, encouraging me to contribute further to society in the future, both in terms of mitigation and adaption.

On sustainability, I completed a project on sustainable urban drainage systems as a mitigation strategy for pluvial flooding in Shanghai, using both computer models (SWMM, a distributed rainfall-runoff model) and empirical methods.
